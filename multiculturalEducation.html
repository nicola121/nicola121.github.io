I made a multicultural education lesson plan below for middle school math. This artifact demonstrates my ability to incorporate many different perspectives and cultures into my lesson planning and teaching. I want to make sure that the diversity of my students is valued; I plan to involve students' cultures, hobbies, and home lives in how I teach in the classroom because it will help the students feel valued and also help each of us learn something new! There are equitable opportunities for all students to learn mathematics. These concepts can be found in the lesson plan below.

It's also important to focus on how math evolved through time by people like us, though with many different cultural backgrounds. I really enjoyed talking about fractions and how they were originially created to help people express sharing food with each other evenly. It makes mathematicians a little less far off and scary.
